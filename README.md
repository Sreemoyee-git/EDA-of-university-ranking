# EDA-of-university-ranking

University Ranking Analysis

Problem Statement:

The task is to analyze the global rankings of universities, focusing on the number of universities in the top rankings and their corresponding world ranks. The project aims to uncover insights from the data on how the number of universities and their world ranks correlate with each other. This analysis is crucial for understanding the performance of countries and universities in global rankings, as well as identifying patterns in research and citations.

What I Did:

1. Data Collection & Cleaning:

The first step involved importing and cleaning the dataset, which contained the rankings of universities from multiple countries.

Null values were handled, and any inconsistent data entries were corrected to ensure the integrity of the analysis.

2. Exploratory Data Analysis:

Conducted an exploratory data analysis to understand the distribution and trends in university rankings, focusing on the top countries with the highest number of ranked universities.

Calculated key statistics to examine the relationship between citations and world rankings, as citations are a major factor in global academic rankings.

3. Visualization:

Created several visualizations to illustrate the insights:

Bar Chart: Visualizing the top 10 countries by number of ranked universities, highlighting the USAâ€™s dominance.

Horizontal Bar Chart: Showing the top 10 countries by average world rank, where lower ranks (closer to 1) are better, indicating countries that perform well on average in global university rankings.

Line Chart: Analyzing ranking trends over time for top universities, showing how their rankings have changed across the years.

Scatter Plot with Regression: Examining the correlation between world rank and citations, providing insights into the influence of citations on rankings.


Tools Used:

Python

Libraries:
Pandas for data manipulation and cleaning.

Matplotlib and Seaborn for data visualization.

Jupyter Notebook for running and documenting the analysis.

Recommendation:

Focus on High-Performing Countries: The analysis highlights USA as a clear leader in terms of both the number of ranked universities and average rank. This suggests that resources and attention should be directed to countries with strong research infrastructures, particularly the USA, China, and the UK. Universities in these regions can serve as models for best practices.

Improve Citation Impact: The scatter plot shows a clear correlation between world rank and citations, with higher-ranked universities generally having more citations. Therefore, universities looking to improve their rankings should focus on increasing their research output and citations, as these are highly influential in global rankings.

Focus on Emerging Markets: Countries like South Africa, Norway, and Hong Kong have relatively better average world ranks, which indicates their education systems may have high research productivity but fewer institutions. This can be a point of growth for other countries looking to improve their global standing.


Conclusion:

This analysis provides valuable insights into the global rankings of universities. By focusing on the number of universities and their average world ranks, the project identified key trends in how countries perform globally. The strong correlation between citations and world rank suggests that universities must prioritize research and citation impact to improve their global standing.
